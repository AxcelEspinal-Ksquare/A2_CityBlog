Santo Domingo

What do I enjoy about this city?

History

This city was first populated by the Taínos, a native group of people with rudimentary lifestyles. After the Colonization (1492), when Columbus arrived to this isle, spanish people came in with african slaves and forced us to change our culture.

Santo Domingo served as some sort of headquarters for Spain regarding the colonization of all of Latin America.

Food

Watch out for Mofongo!

Althought not born in Santo Domingo, the Mofongo has widely popularized between the citizens of this city, thanks to chains like Mofongo El Mocano and La Esquina del Chicharrón.

Mofongo is pretty simple to make. Originally, it consists of barely fried mashed green plantains, with a lot of garlic and pieces of chicharrón (fried pork belly). Some other variations have come around, like adding cheese or mushroom sauce.

Points of interest in Santo Domingo

Where should you go?

Being the biggest city in the Dominican Republic, there is a lot to do in Santo Domingo! Here are some of the most interesting things to look out for:

  1. Zona Colonial: A magical place full of colonial history. Live music, museums and restaurants make this a must-go!
  2. Malecón: Very close to Zona Colonial is the Malecón, where you can enjoy the waves and some fun activities, like having an ice cream by the shore.
  3. Parque Iberoamericano: Host of the National Music Conservatorium, this parks offers many different enviroments to enjoy with your loved ones.
  4. Plaza de la Cultura: As stated by its name, this is a place to the cults. It hosts a museum and the National Theather.
  5. Jardín Botánico:A beautiful garden that hosts some very interesting plants, flowers and different envitoments to breathe some fresh air.
